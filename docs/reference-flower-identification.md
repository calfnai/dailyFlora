# DailyFlora Reference Flower Identification

This is a visual identification pass over `docs/dailyflora-reference-gallery.html` and its local images.

The goal is not botanical certainty from small social-media screenshots. The goal is to name the visible flower/material roles clearly enough that DailyFlora can generate, evaluate, and revise bouquets with accountable flower plans.

## Overall Flower Vocabulary

- **Disk / face flowers**: gerbera-like daisies, chamomile, daisy, sunflower, anemone/cosmos-like open flowers.
- **Layered round flowers**: rose, ranunculus, lisianthus-like blooms, camellia/peony/pompon chrysanthemum-like heads.
- **Spike / vertical flowers**: delphinium/larkspur-like blue spikes, foxtail lily/eremurus-like tall spikes, snapdragon/hyacinth-like dense vertical flower clusters, liatris-like wands.
- **Open sculptural flowers**: orchid-like asymmetric petals, narcissus-like trumpet centers, calla-like curled cups, tulip/lily-like pointed petals.
- **Cluster flowers**: hydrangea-like cloud clusters, small pompon mums, dense mini-flower bunches.
- **Fruit / berry elements**: berry dots, bell-fruit-like capsules, small colored round fruit accents.
- **Air / filler materials**: baby's breath, rice flower, waxflower-like tiny dots, thin branch sprays, fern/grass/needle-leaf textures, vine-like outer lines.

## Positive References

### Summer Dopamine

Likely visible materials:

- Yellow daisy/gerbera-like disk flowers.
- Blue delphinium/larkspur-like vertical florets.
- Pale purple carnation/lisianthus-like ruffled flowers.
- Orange/pink small open flowers, possibly cosmos or butterfly-ranunculus-like.
- Green bell or fruit-like accents.
- Baby's breath / rice-flower-like small white dots.
- Thin outer branch sprays.

Generator meaning:

- Needs multiple flower roles at once: disk flowers, blue spikes, ruffled round flowers, fruit dots, airy filler.
- The color can be bright, but the structure must stay light and vertical.

### Berry Grove / 浆果森林

Likely visible materials:

- Many small multi-color open flowers.
- Berry-like round dots and small fruit accents.
- Small daisies / chamomile-like faces.
- Fine green foliage and tiny filler flowers.
- Transparent wrapping that makes the group read as a gathered small-flower bundle.

Generator meaning:

- Should not be dominated by roses or one main bloom.
- Needs small fruit dots, tiny flowers, and leafy texture.

### Autumn Juice

Likely visible materials:

- Orange/yellow ranunculus or rose-like layered blooms.
- White lisianthus/rose-like round blooms.
- Blue small cluster flowers, possibly delphinium/hydrangea-like accents.
- Small chamomile/daisy faces.
- Warm fruit-like accents.
- Cream wrapping that increases the warm juice feeling.

Generator meaning:

- Warm layered blooms plus small blue/white cooling accents.
- Fruitiness should be visible, but not become a fruit basket.

### Romance Made Visible

Likely visible materials:

- Roses and ranunculus-like layered flowers.
- Pink orchid-like or butterfly-shaped open flowers.
- Yellow round blooms.
- White hydrangea/cluster filler.
- Red berry accents.
- Fern-like and bright green support material.

Generator meaning:

- Needs polished bouquet completeness: main layered flowers, secondary sculptural flowers, berry dots, green support.
- Avoid single oversized flower dominance.

### Cappuccino Cream

Likely visible materials:

- Cream, beige, peach, and soft orange open/layered flowers.
- Daisy/gerbera-like face flowers.
- Small blue accent flowers.
- Fine white filler.

Generator meaning:

- A low-contrast warm palette.
- Flower forms should be soft and rounded, not sharp or neon.

### Volume Read

Likely visible materials:

- Large yellow sunflower/gerbera-like disk flower.
- Blue delphinium-like flowers.
- White daisies.
- Yellow roses or small ranunculus.
- Green branch leaves.

Generator meaning:

- Useful as a volume lesson, but the oversized sunflower is a risk.
- Main flowers must not flatten the whole bouquet.

## New Overall + Detail References

### Summer Pinwheel

Likely visible materials:

- Orange starburst/daisy-like flowers with long narrow petals.
- Blue small flowers.
- White umbel/filler clusters.
- Orange bell or fruit-like accents.
- Tall green stems and branch sprays.
- Dense fine greenery.

Generator meaning:

- Must have identifiable star/daisy forms and outer branching.
- Good for "whole shape plus local detail".

### Fairy Violet

Likely visible materials:

- Blue-purple delphinium/larkspur-like vertical flowers.
- Purple lisianthus/rose-like round flowers.
- White baby's breath/rice-flower sprays.
- Long thin black/green branch arcs.
- Soft blue and lavender filler.

Generator meaning:

- Needs air and negative space; purple should be broken by small white dots and thin lines.
- Avoid heavy purple clumps.

### Narcissus Season

Likely visible materials:

- White/yellow narcissus-like trumpet flowers.
- Orange small flowers.
- White baby’s breath or rice-flower sprays.
- Purple small accents.
- Long narrow green leaves.
- Fine grass/needle textures.

Generator meaning:

- Needs trumpet/open-center flowers, not generic petals.
- White/yellow seasonal identity plus airy branches.

### Breathing Landscape

Likely visible materials:

- Yellow calla-like curved flowers.
- Blue small flowers.
- Yellow tiny filler flowers.
- Green hydrangea/cluster texture.
- Tall grass, fern, and leafy stems.
- Vase-like open spatial composition.

Generator meaning:

- Green plant material is a protagonist, not just leaves.
- Needs front/back depth and open air.

### Foxtail Lily

Likely visible materials:

- Tall foxtail lily / eremurus-like spikes.
- Yellow small filler flowers.
- Blue-purple small flowers.
- White tiny filler dots.
- Branching green leaves.
- Dense lower bouquet support.

Generator meaning:

- Strong vertical spikes are essential.
- Base still needs small flowers and foliage so it is not just tall rods.

### Color Burst Bouquet

Likely visible materials:

- Tulip/lily-like pointed petals.
- Pink and yellow open flowers.
- Blue small flowers.
- White branch spray.
- Long outer stems.
- Mixed small filler.

Generator meaning:

- Allows many colors, but each color needs a role.
- Needs pointed sculptural flowers plus small connectors.

### Blueberry Monotone

Likely visible materials:

- Purple/blue round blooms.
- Berry-like dark dots.
- Thin white branch sprays.
- Green wispy grass.
- Small clustered filler.

Generator meaning:

- Shape is useful; color range is too narrow.
- Keep the structure, add small warm or pale highlights.

## Negative / Caution References

### Bare Bouquet

Visible materials:

- Sunflower.
- Blue hydrangea/delphinium-like flowers.
- White daisies.
- Yellow rose-like blooms.
- Loose green leaves.

Why it is a caution:

- Too raw and sparse.
- Feels less composed; lacks the layered polish of the target references.

### Oversized Sunflower Fault

Visible materials:

- Large sunflower/disk flower.
- White daisies.
- Blue small flowers.
- Yellow rose-like flowers.

Why it is a caution:

- One main flower can dominate the whole image.
- DailyFlora should allow main flowers, but constrain their size and central dominance.

### Overgrown Vase Fault

Visible materials:

- Calla/lily-like white sculptural flowers.
- Pale blue small flowers.
- White daisy-like faces.
- Sparse leaves.

Why it is a caution:

- Shape feels overextended and less balanced.
- Color is too pale and singular without enough rhythm.

## Generator Changes Implied

- Every high-detail bouquet should expose named flower roles before rendering.
- The renderer should separate **flower type** from **placement role**.
- `省` should be low-poly spherical flower masses.
- `清` should be smooth translucent spherical flower masses, not five-petal flowers.
- `精` should be the only mode that attempts explicit botanical silhouettes.
- Five-petal flowers should not be used as the default "clear" representation.
